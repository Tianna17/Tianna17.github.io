Who is BTS?

BTS, also known as the Bangtan Boys, is a seven-member South Korean boy band that began formation in 2010 and debuted in June 2013 under Big Hit Entertainment. The group is comprised of Kim Namjoon (RM), Kim Seakjin (Jin), Min Yoogi (Suga), Jung Hoseok (J-Hope), Park Jimin (Jimin), Kim Taehyung (V), and Jeon Jungkook (Jungkook). The group mainly writes and produces much of their music. They originally debuted as a hip hop group, their musical style has evolved to include a wide range of genres. Their lyrics, often focused on personal and social commentary, touch on the themes of mental health, troubles of school-age youth, loss, the journey towards loving oneself, and individualism.Their work features references to literature and psychological concepts and includes an alternative universe storyline. The group has released several albums and performed on several world tours.

Fun Facts about BTS

  • BTS stands for Bangtan Sonyeondan (방탄소년단) meaning ‘Bulletproof Boy Scouts’ or ‘Beyond the Scene.’
  • Fans who support BTS are called ARMY, an acronym for Adorable Representative M.C. for Youth.
  • BTS has an anti-violence campaign partnership with UNICEF.
  • In March 2016, Forbes listed BTS as the most re-tweeted artist.
  • The first album released by BTS was “Dark and Wild” which came out on August 19, 2014.
  • BTS hold a leading role in producing most of their own music.
  • BTS performed at the 2017 American Music Awards which was their first ever TV performance in the United States.
  • BTS has sold at least 7 million albums globally as of July 2018.
  • BTS donated seven tons of rice to charity in 2016 at the K-Star Road opening ceremony.
